What is it?

A whole convoy of kids, rolling to school.

The Scotts Park Bike Bus is a parent-led group that cycles to Scotts Park Primary together as one happy, high-viz convoy. Safety in numbers, a fun start to the day, and one less car on the school run.

Heading the same way to St. Joseph's or Breaside? Come ride along with us - you're very welcome to join.

How we ride

🧒
Kids on the pavement
The children cycle together on the pavement, kept safely off the road.
👋
Parents on the road
Grown-ups ride on the road alongside, shepherding the group and keeping everyone visible.
🤝
We all stick together
No one gets left behind - we move as one bus from start to finish.

Why join?

It's free, it's friendly, and it's a brilliant way to build your child's confidence on a bike - plus a cheerful, healthy start to a Friday for the whole family.

The route

One line, all the way to school.

We follow the same friendly route every Friday - start at the meeting point and roll together down to Scotts Park Primary. Live a little closer? Hop on at one of the stops along the way.

🚲 0.9 miles ⏱️ ~12 min at kid pace ⛰️ Mostly flat
  1. 8:25 The Meet Wheels up

    Meeting point at ///unfair.chins.icons (bottom of King's Meadow, north side). Gather from 8:15.

  3. ~8:33 Second pickup Join here

    ///market.filled.nights · Same again - be there or we carry on without you.

  4. ~8:37 Scotts Park Primary Arrive

    Orchard Road, Bromley BR1 2PR. Bikes parked, ready for the gates to open.

⏰ Please be on time. At the pickup stops we don't wait - if you're not there, we'll keep rolling and you can catch us further along or meet us at school. Times are approximate, so aim to be at your stop a couple of minutes early and listen for the bells!

Before you ride

A few simple rules to keep us safe.

!
Confident riders onlyKids must be able to ride without stabilisers for at least 1 mile.
!
Parents accompany their kidsEvery child must be accompanied by their own grown-up.
✓
We all stick togetherWe ride as one group, at the group's pace.
★
Hi-viz recommendedFor parents and kids alike - be seen, be safe.
★
Bells, please!Highly recommended - a friendly ring keeps everyone aware.
★
Roadworthy bikesBrakes that work and tyres pumped up - give it a quick check.

The three golden rules

Easy to remember, easy to follow.

  1. No racing
  2. Stick together
  3. Have fun!
